Email Acceptable Use Policy

Last Update Status: June 2021

1. Overview

This document serves to supplement the Email Policy. This policy sets further guidelines for the use of email.

2. Purpose

2.a Email is provided to all Property.CoZa users to assist them in carrying out their duties efficiently and effectively. Email enables effective and efficient communication with other members of staff, other companies and partner organizations

2.b This policy is in place to ensure effective use of time, to prevent illegal and inappropriate use of email.

3. Scope

3.a This policy applies to the use of any email sent from a Property.CoZa email address and applies to all employees, vendors, contractors, consultants, and agents operating on behalf of Property.CoZa including all personnel affiliated with third parties.

3.b All users of the organisation’s IT facilities must understand and use this policy. Users are responsible for ensuring the safety and security of the organisation’s systems and the information that they use or manipulate.

3.c All users have a role to play and a contribution to make to the safe and secure use of email.

4. Policy

4.a Corporate policy on use of email

4.a.i The organisation's email facilities are primarily for business use.

4.a.ii Occasional and reasonable personal use of email is permitted in your own time subject to the conditions set out in the organisation’s security policies.

4.a.iii When using the organisation’s electronic mail facilities you should comply with the following guidelines below.

4.a.iv Corporate email filters are utilized to prevent emails being delivered which may contain inappropriate or malicious content.

4.a.v Emails that need to be accessed to conduct the organization’s business but are blocked can be made available by contacting Email Support. Authorization will be required before access is granted.

4.a.vi Accidental viewing of materials that infringes this policy should be reported according to the information security incident reporting procedure.

4.a.vii Only users who have been authorized to use email @propertycoza.co.za may do so.

4.a.viii Users who use company email without authorization, or who provide unauthorized access, may have disciplinary action taken against them.

4.b Monitoring of Email Use

4.b.i All electronic mail coming into or leaving the organization is scanned for viruses and offensive material.

4.b.ii The use of email is recorded and may be monitored. It is possible to identify the senders, recipients, and content of email.

4.b.iii The organization reserves the right to inspect any files at any time during investigations where there is suspected misuse and to withdraw access to the user of the email.

4.c Personal Use of Email

4.c.i Personal use is defined as any activity that is not work-related or necessary in the performance of duties connected to your employment.

4.c.ii Users may use, on an occasional basis, the organization’s computers for personal use to send and receive email.

4.c.iii Users who utilize one of the organization’s computers for personal use to send and receive email access must accept, as a condition of doing so, that their activity may be monitored.

4.c.iv Users using the organization’s computers waive any rights to privacy regarding personal information on the organization’s computers.

4.c.v The personal use of email must not be excessive. It should not count as working time and must not interfere or detract from the organization’s business or work. It should not distract any such individual from their work.

4.c.vi Users should seek to keep any costs incurred because of personal use of email to a minimum.

4.c.vii No liability can be accepted by the organization for any loss that an individual may suffer because of personal use of the organization’s computers and systems.

4.c.viii Support must not be requested from other employees for personal use of email.

4.c.ix Subscription to email mailing lists or list servers for personal purposes is not allowed.

4.c.x Using email for personal purposes must comply with the principles set out in this security policy. Failure to comply with the policy may lead to disciplinary action and deactivation of the organisations email service.

4.d Phishing

4.d.i Take care when viewing emails not to be deceived by phishing. Reputable organizations will not ask you to run software or click on a link to verify your password.

4.e Purchasing of Goods or Services

4.e.i The purchasing of goods or services via email is subject to the organization’s financial regulations. These must be consulted to determine which goods and services it is permissible to purchase.

4.f Instant Messaging

4.f.i The use of Internet Instant Messaging tools is forbidden from the organization’s PCs and Networks. The lack of formal controls concerning data transfer and logging of messages prohibit their use.

4.g Computer Viruses and Malicious Programs

4.g.i Computers can be infected by viruses and malicious programs by opening an attachment to an email or just visiting a link to a webpage contained within the email.

4.g.ii If you think you have a computer virus, report it to your Office Administrator and/or supervisor immediately.

4.h Masquerading

4.h.i It is an offence to masquerade as another person via email and to send email in another person’s name and will be viewed as non-comliance.

4.i Legal Compliance

4.i.i The use of email must be for lawful purposes only, and must comply with relevant legislation.

4.i.ii You will be placing yourself at risk of prosecution if unlawful action is involved.

4.i.iii Electronic communications and files are admissible in court as evidence. Do not write anything about anybody that you cannot prove (with evidence).

4.j Security

4.j.i Users should note that email is not inherently secure. Most email transmitted over the internet are sent in plain text. This means that they are vulnerable to interception.

4.j.ii Although such interceptions are rare, it is best to regard email as an open communication system.

4.j.iii Do not open email attachments from unknown sources as they may contain a virus, trojan, spyware, or other malware.

4.j.iv Never disable security or email scanning software. These tools are essential to protect the business from all security problems.

4.j.v Users must not send confidential company data via email. The IT department can advise on appropriate tools use instead.

4.j.vi If access of another person’s company email account is required, for instance, while an employee is off sick, they should approach their immediate supervisor or Office Administrator to make an official request.

DO:

  • Do check your electronic mail daily to see if you have any messages.
  • Do check the address line before sending a message and check you are sending it to the right person.
  • Do delete electronic mail messages when they are no longer required.
  • Do respect the legal protections to data and software provided by copyright and licenses.
  • Do take care not to express views, which could be regarded as defamatory or libelous.

DO NOT:

  • Do not print electronic mail messages unless absolutely necessary.
  • Do not expect an immediate reply, the recipient may not be at there computer or could be too busy to reply straight away.
  • Do not forward electronic mail messages sent to you personally to others, especially without the permission of the originator.
  • Do not send excessively large electronic mail messages or attachments.
  • Do not send unnecessary messages such as festive greetings or other non-work related items by electronic mail, particularly to several people.
  • Do not participate in chain or pyramid messages or similar schemes.
  • Do not represent yourself as another person.
  • Do not use electronic mail to send or forward material that could be construed as confidential, political, obscene, threatening, offensive, or defamatory.

5. Policy Compliance

(5.1) Compliance Measurement

Property.CoZa will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, periodic walk-throughs, video monitoring, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

(5.2) Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved by Property.CoZa in advance.

(5.3) Non-Compliance

Any persons found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment / independent contractor agreement.
