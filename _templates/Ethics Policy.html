Ethics Policy

Last Update Status: June 2021

1. Overview

Property.CoZa is committed to protecting employees, partners, vendors and the company from illegal or damaging actions by individuals, either knowingly or unknowingly. When Property.CoZa addresses issues proactively and uses correct judgment, it will help set us apart from competitors.

Property.CoZa will not tolerate any wrongdoing or impropriety at any time. Property.CoZa will take the appropriate measures and act quickly to correct the issue if the ethical code is broken.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a culture of openness, trust and to emphasize the employee’s and consumer’s expectation to be treated to fair business practices. This policy will serve to guide business behavior to ensure ethical conduct. Effective ethics is a team effort involving the participation and support of every person contracted to Property.CoZa and all personnel should familiarize themselves with the ethics guidelines that follow this introduction.

3. Scope

This policy applies to employees, contractors, consultants, temporaries, and other workers at Property.CoZa, including all personnel affiliated with third parties.

4. Policy

4.a Executive Commitment to Ethics

4.a.i Senior leaders and executives within Property.CoZa must set a prime example. In any business practice, honesty and integrity must be top priority for executives.

4.a.ii Executives must have an open door policy and welcome suggestions and concerns from personnel. This will allow personnel to feel comfortable discussing any issues and will alert executives to concerns within the work force.

4.a.iii Executives must disclose any conflict of interests regarding their position within Property.CoZa.

4.b Employee Commitment to Ethics

4.b.i Property.CoZa personnel will treat everyone fairly, have mutual respect, promote a team environment and avoid the intent and appearance of unethical or compromising practices.

4.b.ii All personnel need to apply effort and intelligence in maintaining ethical values.

4.b.iii Personnel must disclose any conflict of interests regard their position within Property.CoZa.

4.b.iv Personnel will help Property.CoZa to increase customer, partner, and vendor satisfaction by providing quality service and timely response to inquiries.

4.b.v Personnel should consider the following questions to themselves when any behavior is questionable:

4.b.v.1 Is the behavior legal?

4.b.v.2 Does the behavior comply with all appropriate Property.CoZa policies?

4.b.v.3 Does the behavior reflect Property.CoZa values and culture?

4.b.v.4 Could the behavior adversely affect company stakeholders?

4.b.v.5 Would you feel personally concerned if the behavior appeared in a news headline?

Could the behavior adversely affect Property.CoZa if all personnel did it?

4.c Company Awareness

4.c.i Property.CoZa will promote a trustworthy and honest culture to reinforce the vision of ethics within the company.

4.d Maintaining Ethical Practices

4.d.i Property.CoZa will reinforce the importance of the integrity message and the tone will start at the top. All personnel, managers and directors need to consistently maintain an ethical stance and support ethical behavior.

4.d.ii Personnel at Property.CoZa should encourage open dialogue, get honest feedback and treat everyone fairly, with honesty and objectivity.

4.d.iii Employees are required to recertify their compliance to Ethics Policy by resigning this document and any amendments made on an annual basis.

4.e Unethical Behavior

4.e.i Property.CoZa will avoid the intent and appearance of unethical or compromising practice in relationships, actions and communications.

4.e.ii Property.CoZa will not tolerate harassment or discrimination.

4.e.iii Unauthorized use of company trade secrets & marketing, operational, personnel, financial, source code, & technical information integral to the success of our company will not be tolerated.

4.e.iv Property.CoZa will not permit impropriety at any time and we will act ethically and responsibly in accordance with laws.

4.e.v Property.CoZa employees will not use corporate assets or business relationships for personal use or gain.

5. Policy Compliance

(5.1) Compliance Measurement

Property.CoZa will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback.

(5.2) Exceptions

None.

(5.3) Non-Compliance

Any persons found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment / independent contractor agreement.
