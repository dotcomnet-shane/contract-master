Information Backups Policy

Last Update Status: June 2021

1. Overview

Electronic Information can be lost as a result of crashed disks, deletion, or corruption. Therefore, integrity and availability of important electronic information needs to be maintained by making regular copies to other media.

2. Purpose

2.a This policy defines the strategy for backing up the organisation’s information and software application systems.

2.b The aim of the policy is to ensure that it is always possible to recover the information and application systems.

3. Scope

3.a This policy applies to all electronic information stored upon the organisation’s servers, PC’s and laptops.

3.b The policy also applies to all application systems, the application software and its configuration.

4. Policy

4.a Backup Method

4.a.i Servers and systems will be backed up using a suitable backup method. An appropriate storage medium will be used. This may be tape, online Internet backup, mirrored servers at a remote site, or any other recognised medium.

4.a.ii Backups will be performed using dedicated backup software appropriate for the operating system being used.

4.b Backup & Restore Procedures

4.b.i The servers and systems will be backed up using the standard facilities available within the backup software being used.

4.b.ii Documentation with sufficient detail to allow an experienced user of the backup software to restore data will be produced.

4.c Backup Status

4.c.i The backup software will be configured to automatically alert an administrator as to the status of any backup performed.

4.c.ii The backup status will be reviewed daily and any faults identified will be rectified.

4.d Verification and Restore Testing

4.d.i Where possible the backup software will be configured to automatically verify the backup. The verification will be accomplished by comparing the contents of the backup to the data on disk.

4.d.ii The restoration of information from backup will be tested periodically.

4.e Backup Cycles

4.e.i Data Repository

Where possible a full backup of important systems will be taken every day. If there is insufficient available time to perform a full backup, then at a minimum a full backup will be taken weekly, with incremental backups being taken every day.

4.e.ii Rotation Scheme

Backups will utilise the grandfather, father, son (GFS) method of daily, weekly and monthly backups or a simple daily rotation method. Where a simple daily rotation method is used, at a minimum ten (10) backups will be kept.

4.e.iii Daily Backups

Daily backups will consist of backup taken every day as part of a simple daily rotation or as part of a GFS rotation scheme.

4.e.iv A daily backup will consist of either a full backup or an incremental / differential backup.

4.e.v Weekly Backups

Weekly backups will consist of a 5 tape set with the tape being changed on a weekly basis.

4.e.vi A weekly backup will consist of a full backup.

4.e.vii Monthly Configuration Backup

Monthly configuration backups will consist of exporting or backing up the configuration settings of an application. The configuration will be stored on a server that is backed up daily.

4.f Backup Storage

4.f.i Backup media will be securely stored when not in use.

4.f.ii Online and remote disk mirroring backups will be held at a data centre at least 2km away from the data centre containing the information being backed up.

4.f.iii Removable media such as tapes will be securely stored in a fire safe when not in use.

4.f.iv For resilience several removable media will be stored offsite.

4.f.v At a minimum two copies of the most up to date backups will be stored offsite twice a week.

4.f.vi The schedule for offsite storage will be detailed in a log.

4.g Tape Drive Cleaning

4.g.i Tape drives used for backups will be cleaned on a regular basis.

4.g.ii Autoloader tape drives will be cleaned weekly via a cleaning tape in the device.

4.g.iii Other tape drives will be cleaned monthly.

4.h Application Backup

4.h.i Use will be made of online backup techniques where they are available to minimise downtime.

4.h.ii Full offline backups will be utilised where online backups are not available.
